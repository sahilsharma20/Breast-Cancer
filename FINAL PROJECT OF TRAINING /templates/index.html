Educational Information

What is Breast Cancer?

Breast cancer is a type of cancer that forms in the cells of the breasts. It is one of the most common cancers in women, though it can also occur in men. It starts when cells in the breast begin to grow uncontrollably, forming a tumor.

Symptoms of Breast Cancer

Common symptoms of breast cancer include a lump in the breast or underarm area, changes in the size or shape of the breast, discharge from the nipple, or skin changes. It's important to consult a healthcare provider if you notice any of these symptoms.

Steps of Treatment

Treatment for breast cancer often includes surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, and hormone therapy. The specific treatment plan depends on the stage and type of breast cancer and the patient's overall health.

Prevention

Preventive measures include regular screening, maintaining a healthy weight, exercising regularly, and limiting alcohol consumption. Genetic testing may also be recommended for individuals with a family history of breast cancer.

Stages of Breast Cancer

Breast cancer stages range from Stage 0 (in situ) to Stage IV (advanced). Staging is based on the size of the tumor, whether cancer has spread to lymph nodes or other parts of the body, and other factors.
