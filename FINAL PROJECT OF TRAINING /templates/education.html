Educational Information

Learn more about breast cancer, its symptoms, prevention, treatment, and stages.

What is Breast Cancer?

Breast cancer occurs when cells in the breast begin to grow uncontrollably. These cells form a tumor that can often be seen on an X-ray or felt as a lump.

Symptoms of Breast Cancer

  • Presence of a lump or mass in the breast or underarm area.
  • Changes in breast size or shape.
  • Changes in the appearance of the breast or nipple skin.
  • Unusual discharge from the nipple.

Steps of Treatment

Treatment for breast cancer usually involves a combination of surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, hormone therapy, and targeted therapy. The treatment plan depends on the type and stage of cancer.

Prevention

Preventive measures include maintaining a healthy weight, staying physically active, limiting alcohol intake, and undergoing regular screenings.

Stages of Breast Cancer

Breast cancer stages range from 0 (carcinoma in situ) to IV (metastatic breast cancer). The stage depends on the tumor size, lymph node involvement, and the extent of spread to other parts of the body.
